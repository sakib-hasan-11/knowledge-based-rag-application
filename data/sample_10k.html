Item 1 Business

Apple Inc. designs, manufactures, and markets smartphones, personal computers, tablets, wearables, and accessories worldwide. The Company's products include iPhone, Mac, iPad, Apple Watch, and AirPods. Apple is vertically integrated with extensive software development and hardware engineering capabilities. The iPhone segment is our largest revenue generator, accounting for roughly half of our total revenue. The Services segment, which includes the App Store, Apple Music, iCloud, and Apple TV+, represents our fastest-growing business. Our retail distribution includes both company-operated stores and third-party retailers. We maintain strong brand equity and customer loyalty through superior product design and seamless integration across our ecosystem.

Item 1A Risk Factors

The following risk factors may materially affect our business: Supply Chain Risk: We rely on providers in Asia for component manufacturing and assembly. Disruptions in these regions could impact our ability to meet demand. Foreign Exchange Risk: Approximately 40% of our revenue is generated internationally. Fluctuations in foreign exchange rates could negatively impact gross margins. Competition: We face intense competition from Samsung, Google, and other manufacturers offering similar products at lower price points. Regulatory and Legal Risk: We face ongoing regulatory scrutiny regarding privacy, antitrust, and labor practices in various jurisdictions. Technology Risk: Rapid technological change could render our products obsolete if we fail to innovate.

Item 7 Management's Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations

Overview: Our fiscal 2023 revenue was $394.3 billion, up 2% from fiscal 2022. Net income was $96.9 billion, down 1% year-over-year. iPhone Revenue: iPhone revenue declined 2% to $200.6 billion due to softer consumer demand in China and macroeconomic headwinds. However, we saw strong performance from iPhone 15 Pro in developed markets. Services Revenue: Services grew 16% to $85.2 billion, driven by growth in iCloud, Apple Music subscriptions, and advertising services. Services now represent 21.6% of total revenue, up from 19.8% in fiscal 2022. Gross Margin: Gross margin declined to 46.2% from 46.4%, primarily due to unfavorable product mix and supply chain costs, partially offset by successful pricing actions on certain products. Operating Expenses: R&D spending increased 12% to $30.7 billion to support new product development and AI initiatives. SG&A expenses declined 1% to $13.9 billion due to controlled spending growth.
